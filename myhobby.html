Crochet

Crochet is a needlecraft, which uses a hook to interconnect loops of yarn to form a textile. The word "crochet" derives from old French and Germanic words meaning "hook". There is evidence of knitted textiles dating back to Ancient Egypt, but modern crochet seems to have started in the nineteenth century. The method can be used to create a wide variety of patterns, but found particular popularity for lace-making. This was hugely important in Ireland during the Great Famine (1845-1849) as the production of Irish Crochet Lace was used as part of the Famine Relief Schemes. During the 1960s and 1970s, crocheted clothes became very fashionable.

The recent resurgence in 1970s fashions, coupled with the popularity of crafting during the pandemic, have brought crochet to new generations. Crochet is especially in vogue recently given viral TikTok patterns for hats and bags, as well as the appearance of crocheted designs in the Loewe, JW Anderson and Michael Kors 2024 Spring/Summer collections. Crochet is no longer just for grannies! But where can you learn to crochet? There are a wealth of resources available online, with hundreds of tutorials available on YouTube. Once you have the hang of it, sign up for free for Ravelry for access to thousands of patterns, to chat to the community and for other resources. Happy hooking!
